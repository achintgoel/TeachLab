English II Writing

by Anne

Posted on August 24, 2013 at 9:00 PM

  • Syllabus
  • Curriculum
  • Activities

In the second course of the CTW sequence, we will move from examining how discourse functions in our own identity formations to consider discourse as it is formed around larger cultural, political, and geographical issues.

Throughout these reading and writing assignments, we will consider how discourse both addresses controversy and conflict and also becomes a possibility for change. We will study how interventions in discourse can make us view and understand the world around us differently. We will begin by looking at the “borderland” discourse of Gloria Anzaldua. From here, we will move to an analysis of the visual and textual formation of Palestinian identity in Edward Said’s After the Last Sky. Then, we will look at the discourse of social media and social change in the advent of the Arab Spring. As our last writing assignment, we will return back to our own discourse communities to map out a language project around one of the conflicts or controversies that arises in our community.

Throughout the quarter, we will continue our examination and study of elements of rhetorical theory—in particular, the discussion of contemporary rhetoric—to employ a terminology, strategy, and means of viewing the contemporary art of discourse.
